Grand Inquisitor Aurelius the Stern

The man who saved a million souls

    Here’s a time line of Aurelius’ life:

  • 1472 – Born in Toledo, Castile
  • 1487 – Joins the Order of the Holy Flame at age 15 after mistakenly setting his schoolyard on fire during a “witch-detector” experiment
  • 1493 – Studies under Cardinal Montemayor, who teaches him that suspicious hats are often the first sign of sorcery
  • 1499 – Writes his first treatise On the Dangerous Properties of Broomsticks
  • 1502 – Leads his first public lecture on the prophylaxis of witchcraft; insists that garlic necklaces and regular hymn-singing protect the soul
  • 1507 – Appointed Junior Inquisitor after successfully proving that a suspicious goat was merely a goat
  • 1515 – Develops the “Holy Checklist of Thirteen Signs of Heresy,” still copied by village clerks across Castile
  • 1521 – Survives the infamous “Soup Cauldron Panic,” when an entire town believed stew was cursed. Aurelius heroically tasted it first, declaring it “only slightly salty”
  • 1530 – Becomes Grand Inquisitor, expands his doctrine that prevention is better than persecution: “Better to sprinkle holy water on your neighbor’s laundry than to burn their house”
  • 1545 – Publishes The Everyday Manual of Anti-Witchcraft Hygiene, including advice on cleaning brooms, polishing cauldrons, and keeping suspicious cats indoors at night
  • 1552 – Awarded the Golden Censer of Virtue for reducing witchcraft accusations in his diocese by 73% through education and preventive measures
  • 1560 – Declares that laughter is the best counter-spell, institutes weekly Comedy Sermons to disarm fear of sorcery
  • 1567 – Dies at the age of 95, reportedly after blessing an entire vineyard against grape-demons.
