My Favorite Books

Fantasy Science Fiction Non Fiction

My favorite fantasy books

The Hobbit

by J.R.R. Tolkien

A fantasy novel about the adventures of Bilbo Baggins in Middle-earth.

Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone

by J.K. Rowling

The first book in the Harry Potter series, introducing the magical world of Hogwarts.

The Name of the Wind

by Patrick Rothfuss

A tale of a magically gifted young man who grows up to be the most notorious wizard his world has ever seen.

My favorite science fiction books

Dune

by Frank Herbert

A science fiction epic set on the desert planet Arrakis, focusing on politics, religion, and ecology.

Neuromancer

by William Gibson

A cyberpunk novel that explores artificial intelligence and virtual reality.

The Left Hand of Darkness

by Ursula K. Le Guin

A science fiction story set on a planet where the inhabitants can change gender.

My favorite non-fiction books

Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind

by Yuval Noah Harari

An exploration of the history and impact of Homo sapiens on the world.

In Cold Blood

by Truman Capote

On November 15, 1959, in the small town of Holcomb, Kansas, four members of the Clutter family were savagely murdered by blasts from a shotgun held a few inches from their faces. There was no apparent motive for the crime, and there were almost no clues.

Hiroshima

by John Hersey

Hiroshima is the story of six human beings who lived through the greatest single manmade disaster in history. John Hersey tells what these six -- a clerk, a widowed seamstress, a physician, a Methodist minister, a young surgeon, and a German Catholic priest -- were doing at 8:15 a.m. on August 6, 1945, when Hiroshima was destroyed by the first atomic bomb ever dropped on a city. Then he follows the course of their lives hour by hour, day by day.
